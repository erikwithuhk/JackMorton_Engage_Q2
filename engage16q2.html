One of the benefits of being a global agency is the chance to experience new cultures and environments either by traveling onsite or meeting with current and potential clients. In some cases, we are able to take this a step further by facilitating short-term and permanent inter-office transfers within our network. For many, this rare opportunity is energizing, sparks creativity, and offers insights and fresh perspectives that make our work extraordinary. In 2015, we supported nearly 20 permanent and 5 short-term transfers. Learn more about inter-office transfers by reaching out to your local HR representative.
#12in16
Add 12 hours of training to your 2016 goals. That’s just one hour per month!
Here are three easy steps to help you reach this goal:
Spend the next 15 minutes exploring the videos, books, webinars, and eLearning available on our Learning Management System.
Create a list of subjects you want to learn more about this year, including those from the LMS, industry talks, networking events, and internal training sessions. Add to this list as things come up or occur to you throughout the year.
Sign up! When your receive the Monthly Training announcement, immediately sign up for one of the webinars listed in the email or identify one hour of training from your list and block your calendar to complete it in that month.
By following these three simple steps, you can make training both a priority and easy. And at the end of the year, you’ll find you achieved those 12 hours and acquired new knowledge to further your career.
 
Things to consider:
• Make a list of all the achievements and accomplishments you had throughout the last year.
• Identify areas where you would like to expand your skills, experience and expertise.
• Think about your goals for 2016 and how they can help you to develop in your career.
Preparing properly for your review will benefit both you and your manager and will result in a more meaningful dialogue around your performance, your goals and your career progression at Jack. Your growth and development are key to your career and essential for our success.
 


Genuine.Does.Good.Shit.
Genuine
–
To kick off this holiday season, Genuine’s volunteer committee, Genuine.Does.Good.Shit. (GDGS), hosted two events to give back to our community:

1. A food drive for the Greater Boston Food Bank, an agency in New England that provided meals to 545,000 people in 2014.Through friendly interoffice competition, Genuine collected nearly 600 items in a week to donate!

2. A blanket making party resulted in 30 fleece tie blankets to donate to the Linus Project. An organization that distributes homemade blankets to children in hospitals, shelters, and social services agencies in all 50 states.

GDGS aims to organize one to two volunteer opportunities per quarter. Not only does it allow us to share with the less fortunate, it also gives us the opportunity to collaborate in different aspect and learn more about each other.
Unlocking #Jackwestside 
West Coast
–
As 2015 came to a close, the West Coast team took time to come together and celebrate an amazing year through their unique initiative Unlocking #Jackwestside.

For 20 days, the team stopped to open a locker and reveal a fun surprise representing a highlight from 2015—from breakfast croissants recognizing our presence at Cannes, to an interactive map showing all the onsite activity to a “Get Inspired” card entitling each person to time and admission to participate in an activity that inspires them, and much more.

Unlocking #Jackwestside was also an opportunity to reflect, recognize and say thanks for a great year by participating in the San Francisco-Marin Food Bank’s annual Food and Fund Drive.
